\section{Introduction}
%------------------------------------------------------------------

A fundamental result in differential geometry states that a manifold admits an 
affine structure if and only if it is locally flat. This equivalence underlies 
the entire theory of Hessian manifolds. The main goal of this paper is to study 
the Ricci curvature of Hessian metrics on the leaves of the foliation generated 
by the first Koszul form $\omega$, and to derive sharp lower and upper bounds 
for the first Betti number of Hessian manifolds with nowhere-vanishing $\omega$.
